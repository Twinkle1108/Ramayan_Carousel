Lord Ram

Lord Rama is the seventh avatar of Vishnu.Rama is known for his courage, loyalty, and devotion. He is also considered the embodiment of righteousness, devotion, and sacrifice. Rama's life story is a reminder of the importance of values and principles in our lives.

Sita

Sita is a goddess born from the earth.She is the consort of Lord Ram. She is also the incarnation of Lakshmi, goddess of prosperity, wealth, and beauty. Sita is portrayed as the ideal wife and woman.She is known for her dedication, self-sacrifice, courage, and purity.

Lakshman

The third son of King Dasharatha was an incarnation of Sesha Naga. He was the twin brother of Shatrughna born to Queen Sumitra. He was the only brother who followed Lord Rama through his exile and battle against Ravana.

Ravan

The son of Vishrava and Kaikashi,Ravana is a 10-headed demon king in Hindu mythology. He is the main antagonist in the epic Ramayana, where he kidnaps Rama's wife Sita. He is often depicted as a powerful and menacing villain, but he is also well respected for his vast knowledge and wisdom.

Hanuman

Hanuman is a devotee of Rama.Hanuman is known for his strength, loyalty, and courage. He is also known for his ability to fly and to change his size.

King Dashratha

King Dasharatha is a character in the Hindu epic Ramayana. He was the king of Kosala and the father of Rama, who is considered to be an incarnation of the Hindu god Vishnu. Dasharatha was a great warrior and a just ruler. He was also a loving father to his sons.

Kausalya

Kausalya is the senior queen of Kosala and wife of King Dasharatha. She is the mother of Rama, the epic's hero. Kausalya is depicted as an ideal mother, wife, and queen. She is wise, compassionate, and devoted to her family. She is also a symbol of endurance and suffering.

Sumitra

Sumitra is a princess of Kashi in Hindu mythology. In the Ramayana, she is one of the three wives of King Dasharatha of Ayodhya and the mother of the twin brothers, Lakshmana and Shatrughna. Sumitra is portrayed as a virtuous and wise queen who loves her sons deeply and guides them on the path of righteousness.

Kaikeyi

Kaikeyi is the third queen and wife of King Dasharatha in the Hindu epic Ramayana. She was the princess of Kekeya, a kingdom that was located in the western part of India. She was also the mother of Prince Bharata. Kaikeyi was known for her strong will and her ability to influence her husband. She was also a skilled warrior and an able counselor to the king. However, she was also known for her jealousy and her ambition.

Bharata

Bharata is a character in the Indian epic Ramayana. He is the second son of King Dasharatha and the brother of Lord Rama. Bharata is known for his righteousness, justice, and wisdom. He is regarded as the incarnation of Panchajanya, the divine conch of Lord Vishnu.

Shatrughan

Shatrughna is the youngest of four brothers and the twin of Lakshmana. His other brothers are Rama and Bharata. He is the son of Queen Sumitra and King Dasharatha of Ayodhya.

Sumantra

Sumantra was the prime minister to King Dasharatha of Ayodhya. He was the most trusted advisor of the king and one of the eight diplomatic artists in the king's court. Sumantra was known for his loyalty to the rulers of Ayodhya and his knowledge of many royal family secrets. He helped Prince Rama in his exile.

Mantara

Manthara is a character in the Hindu epic Ramayana. In the epic, she is described to have convinced Queen Kaikeyi that the throne of Ayodhya belonged to her son Bharata and that her step-son—crown-prince Rama (the protagonist of the Ramayana)—should be exiled from the kingdom.

King Janak

King Janaka is a character in the Hindu epic Ramayana. He was the king of Videha, a region in India, and the adoptive father of Sita. Janaka was known for his wisdom and spirituality, and was considered a Rajarishi, or royal sage. He was also known for his non-attachment to material possessions.

Sunaina

Sunaina is a character in the Ramayana. She was the queen of Mithila, the wife of King Janaka, and the mother of Sita and Urmila.

Urmila

Urmila is a significant character in the Hindu epic Ramayana. She is the daughter of King Janaka of Mithila and Queen Sunayana, and the sister of Sita. She is married to Lakshmana, the younger brother of Lord Rama. Urmila is often referred to as "the forgotten heroine" because of her minor role in the Ramayana. However, her sacrifice is unparalleled.

Mandavi

Mandavi was the wife of Bharat, younger brother if Lord Rama and the elder Princess of Sankashya. She was the incarnation of Mahalakshmi's flower. Her father was King Kushadwaja, younger brother of King Janaka, and Chandrabhaga.

ShrutaKirti

Shrutakirti is a character in the Hindu epic, Ramayana. She is the daughter of King Kushadhvaja and Queen Chandrabhaga, and the younger sister of Sita. Shrutakirti is the wife of Shatrughna, the youngest brother of Rama. She is considered to be an incarnation of the disc of the goddess Lakshmi.

Vali

Vali is a character in the Hindu epic Ramayana. He is the King of Kishkindha, a kingdom of vanaras (monkeys) in the Ramayana. Vali is the son of Indra, the god of thunder, and the elder brother of Sugriva. He is also the husband of Tara and the father of Angada.

Tara

She is the daughter of the monkey physician Sushena, and the wife of the monkey king Vali. Tara and Vali have a son named Angada. Tara is described as a wise woman who always gives good counsel to her husband. She is also a skilled diplomat, and uses her skills to benefit her kingdom. Tara is also a loving mother, and is very close to her son Angada.

Sugriva

Sugriva is the younger brother of Vali, the king of the Vanara people. Sugriva was exiled from the kingdom of Kishkindha by Vali, and lived in exile with his followers at Rsyamukha hill.

Ruma

Ruma was the wife of Sugriva in the Ramayana. She was the daughter of King Panasa, and the mother of three sons. When the two royal Vānara brothers, Sugriva and Vāli, were in conflict, Vāli took Ruma from Sugriva. After Vāli was killed by Rama, Ruma was returned to Sugriva.

Angada

He is the son of the powerful vanara king Vali and Tara. Angada is a vanara prince who is loyal to Lord Rama. He is known for his innocence, might, and righteousness. He is also a great warrior and master of illusions. Angada helps Rama find his wife Sita and fight her abductor, Ravana.

Nala

Nala is a monkey-like Vanara who is credited with building a bridge across the ocean to Lanka. Nala was the son of the architect of the gods, Vishvakarma, and was equal to his father in skill. He was also given a boon by his father. Nala's engineering expertise and dedication were crucial in building the bridge that facilitated Lord Rama's army's passage to Lanka, where Sita was held captive by Ravana. The bridge was also known as Rama Setu or Adam's Bridge. Nala uprooted an entire hill and carried it back to Lanka when he realized time was short.

Neela

He is the commander-in-chief of the monkey army under the monkey king Sugriva and is described as leading the army in Rama's battle against the rakshasa king Ravana of Lanka (identified with modern-day Sri Lanka) and as killing many rakshasas. Though, the Ramayana credits Nala as the sole builder of the Rama Setu, a bridge across the ocean between Rameswaram (India) and Lanka, enabling forces of Rama to pass over to Lanka, other adaptations of the epic attribute the bridge's construction to both Nala and Nila.

Vibhishana

He was the youngest brother of Ravana, the king of the demons, and was known for his righteousness and piety. Vibhishana was a rakshasa (demon) prince, but he was unlike his brothers and sister. He was a clever statesman, and was endowed with great learning.

Mandodari

She is the daughter of the monkey physician Sushena, and the wife of the monkey king Vali. Tara and Vali have a son named Angada. Tara is described as a wise woman who always gives good counsel to her husband. She is also a skilled diplomat, and uses her skills to benefit her kingdom. Tara is also a loving mother, and is very close to her son Angada.

Surphanaka

Surpanakha is a demoness who is the sister of Ravana, the king of Lanka. Her name means "sharp nails" and she is described as having a pot belly, wry eyes, and coppery hair. Surpanakha comes across Rama in the forest and is immediately smitten with his good looks. She asks him to marry her, but Rama refuses, telling her that he is devoted to his wife Sita. Surpanakha is enraged by this rejection and attacks Sita, but Lakshmana, Rama's brother, intervenes and cuts off Surpanakha's nose and ears.

Kumbhakarna

Ruma was the wife of Sugriva in the Ramayana. She was the daughter of King Panasa, and the mother of three sons. When the two royal Vānara brothers, Sugriva and Vāli, were in conflict, Vāli took Ruma from Sugriva. After Vāli was killed by Rama, Ruma was returned to Sugriva.

Meghnad

Meghnad is a major character. He is the eldest son of Ravana and Mandodari, and the crown prince of Lanka. He is also known as Indrajit, which means "Conqueror of Indra." Meghnad is considered one of the greatest warriors in Hindu texts. He is a master of archery and illusion warfare techniques. He can also fight from the sky, hidden behind the clouds.

Trijata

Trijata is a rakshasi (demoness) in the Hindu epic Ramayana who is assigned the duty of guarding Sita who was kidnapped by the demon-king of Lanka Ravana. She is described as a wise old rakshasi, who dreams of Ravana's destruction and the victory of Rama, the husband of Sita who wages war against Ravana to rescue Sita.

Rishi Rishyashringa

The first Rishi who conducted the Putra Kameshthi Yagya, which resulted in Narayana's descent in the world

Rishi Vashishtha

Vashishtha is a revered sage in Hindu traditions and the family priest and preceptor of the Raghu dynasty in the Ramayana. He is the teacher of Lord Rama and his brothers, Lakshmana, Bharata, and Shatrughna. He is also the author of Mandala 7 of the Rigveda.

Rishi Vishwamitra

He is the one who takes Rama and Lakshmana to the forest to protect them from the demoness Tataka. He also teaches them the Bala Atibala Vidya, a secret knowledge that would keep them vigorous and full of vitality.

Rishi Agastya

Agastya is a sage who supports Rama and his brothers in their fight against the demon Ravana. Agastya is short and heavy, but by living in the south, he balances the powers of Shiva and the weight of Mount Kailasha and Mount Meru. Agastya also has the power to transform his body into any form and control the elements.

Rishi Atri

Atri is a Vedic sage in the Hindu religion. He is one of the seven great sages, or Saptarishis, and is the most mentioned sage in the Rig Veda. Atri is also one of the nine Prajapatis, or lords of creation. He is the son of Brahma, the creator god, and his wife, Saraswati, the goddess of knowledge. Atri's wife is Anasuya, who is an embodiment of chastity.

Anasuya

She is the wife of the sage Atri and is known for her purity and chastity. She is also known for her hospitality and kindness, and is visited by Lord Rama, his wife Sita, and his brother Lakshmana during their exile in the forest. When they arrived at her ashram, Anasuya welcomed them warmly and served them with great care and affection.

Ahalya

She was the wife of the sage Gautama Maharishi. According to legend, Indra, the ruler of the gods, was infatuated with Ahalya, so he disguised himself as Gautama and seduced her. When Gautama discovered what had happened, he cursed Ahalya to become a stone. She was freed from the curse by Rama, the seventh avatar of the god Vishnu.
